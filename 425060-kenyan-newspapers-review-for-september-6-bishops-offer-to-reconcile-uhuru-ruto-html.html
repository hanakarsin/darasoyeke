Kenyan Newspapers Review For September 6: William Ruto Blames Uhuru's Gov't for Sabotaging His 2022

Posted by Lourie Helzer on Friday, April 19, 2024

On the Monday of September 6, the national dailies reported on the clash between the Jubilee factions witnessed in Nyeri where Deputy President William Ruto was attending a church service.

The dailies also spotlighted the acrimony between President Uhuru Kenyatta and his deputy, which has hampered cabinet meetings for over six months now.

1. Daily Nation

The publication reported how Ruto's visit to Kieni was met with hostility after a section of local youths tried to block his entry, pelting his motorcade with stones.

Initially, reports emerged that the mainstream churches in the constituency had declined to host the deputy president who had to spend the day at a mini Full-Gospel Church in Mbiriri.

The iron sheet-built church could not accommodate all of Ruto's entourage, resorting to holding the Sunday service at Mbiriri grounds.

However, the security officers present could not allow the pitching of tents at the grounds citing a lack of authorisation.

Ruto's men blamed the day's unfortunate events on Kieni MP Kanini Kega who is a strong ally of the president.

Kega was accused of mobilising goons to disrupt Ruto's visit to the area.

While addressing residents who had gathered by the roadside, Ruto said his journey to state house was on course and would not stumble despite the efforts to derail him.

He suggested the state had embarked on sabotaging his bid but vowed to soldier on unbothered.

2. The Standard

According to The Standard, the Uhuru-led cabinet has not met for over six months now, this being owed to the evident fallout between the president and his deputy.

Joining Uhuru has been a section of his ministers who have openly defied the deputy president.

Recently, the Interior cabinet secretary Fred Matiang'i accused Ruto of sabotaging the government from within.

Matiang'i asked Ruto to resign and officially play the opposition role instead of subverting the government system and efforts from within.

Others who have been on record taking on Ruto is ICT minister Joe Mucheru, who accused the second in command of hoodwinking the youths for his own political advantage.

In light of all these, there is a reported planned reorganisation in the cabinet likely to affect the cabinet members thought to be loyal to the deputy president.

3. People Daily

Owing to the acrimony between Uhuru and Ruto, the clergy in the country has offered to reconcile the two leaders.

Six senior clerics are said to be pushing for a meeting between them and the two leaders this week.

The hardliners surrounding the president and his deputy have been blamed for the deteriorating relationship.

The clerics, Anthony Muheria of Nyeri Catholic Diocese, Silas Yego formerly of AIC, ACK's Jackson Ole Sapit, Philip Anyolo of Kisumu Catholic Diocese, Robert Lang'at of African spell Church and David Oginde formerly of CITAM, will be seeking to reunite the two leaders who have not read from the same script for the past three years.

Ruto has been blaming the handshake which brought a ceasefire between Uhuru and the ODM party leader Raila Odinga.

According to Ruto, Raila hijacked the government ad the agenda it had for the nation, rather initiating the Building Bridges Initiative (BBI) which was quashed by the courts.

4. Taifa Leo

According to the daily, Ruto was turned down by three main churches in Kieni who could not host him for the Sunday, September 5, service.

He spent the day at Mbiriri's Full-Gospel Church, a small establishment that could not accommodate more than 100 people.

Efforts to pitch tents and hold the service outside the church were thwarted by security officers who claimed they had not been notified.

5. The Star

The Star reported on the feuds pitting Ruto's allies in the Mt Kenya region.

The deputy president's allies have accused their counterparts of disloyalty by refusing to fully join UDA.

Gatundu South MP Moses Kuria and Mwangi Kiunjuri, a former CS, are believed to be Ruto's allies but have been adamant to fold their individual parties for the advantage of UDA.

Kuria was categorical that the region still needed a party that would champion the rights of the residents.

He heads the Chama Cha Kazi (CCK) party which is in a working pact with Martha Karua's Narc Kenya and Kiunjuri's The Sevice Party (TSP).
