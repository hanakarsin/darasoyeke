Police Reservist Attacked by Bandits Breaks Down in Hospital: &quot;Mama Analia, Mimi Nalia, Nani Atabaki

Posted by Almeda Bohannan on Wednesday, July 17, 2024
  • The police reservist was patrolling Sosian Ranch with his colleagues when they were attacked by armed bandits
  • He was admitted to Unison Medical Centre in Rumuruti
  • He asked the government to come to their rescue saying they work in poor conditions with no pay

Despite government efforts to restore order in the war-torn Laikipia region, armed bandits are still terrorising residents of Ol-Moran.

A police reservist who was among those injured while patrolling Sosian Ranch broke down while narrating his ordeal with the raiders.

The reservist was admitted to Unison Medical Centre in Rumuruti alongside his colleague and a ranger.

"I am pleading with the national government to deal with these bandits once and for all. Stop giving promises without acting.

"Sasa nimeishi kulia tangu nikiwa mdogo, mimi nalia na mimi ni polisi, mama pia analia, sasa nani atabaki? (I have been crying since I was young...My mother has also been crying for help...If a reservist like me can shed tears then what about the ordinary citizens? he tearfully said according to a video shared by NTV.

The reservist whose name was not revealed disclosed they work in poor conditions with no pay from the government.

"Just imagine volunteering then losing your life in the process, is that justified? The situation is now tricky. I am asking the government to come to our rescue."

One person killed

In the latest attack in the Wangwashi area within Ol Moran in Laikipia county, one person was killed and tens of cattle stolen by bandits.

Eyewitnesses said the attack happened a kilometre away from where the military was digging a trench separating Ol Moran and the Laikipia Nature Conservancy.

"We were feeding our cattle before we were attacked by the bandits when we were heading to the waterhole before returning home. The bandits, unfortunately, killed the old man identified as Maina,'' said Samuel Ruchuiri, a survivor.

The attackers are said to have led the stolen livestock into the vast Laikipia Nature Conservancy.

The area is still under security operation with a 30-day dusk to dawn curfew in effect.

But the locals have expressed dissatisfaction with the conduct of the operation.
