All Kisii county constituencies and their current MPs 2020

Posted by Lourie Helzer on Thursday, June 6, 2024

The 12th parliament has 290 elected members, 47 women representatives and 12 nominated members from different political parties. They serve different legislative roles in parliament, representing the constituencies and counties they were elected from. Kisii County is a populous region with fertile lands. So, how many constituencies are there in Kisii County? The area, part of former Nyanza Province in the Southwestern part of Kenya, possesses 9 constituencies. Who are the Members of Parliament of Kisii County constituencies?

Kisii County covers an area of about 1,302 Km2 and has an average population of more than 1.3 million individuals. Geographically, the County borders Nyamira County to the North East, Migori, and Homabay Counties to the West and Narok to the South.

The County's landscape is amazing, and some of the notable features are the Sameta, Kiamwasi, Nyanchawa and other notable hills that create the terrain that is Kisii County. The region has diverse political affiliation with most Members of Parliament from Kisii County ailing from different political parties. So, who are the current Kisii MPs?

Kisii County constituencies and their Members of Parliament

Agriculture, tourism and hospitality, trade, mining, and livestock are just some of the investment opportunities in Kisii County. All these resources and opportunities are scattered in the vast landscape, politically divided into nine distinct constituencies.

Are you wondering about Kisii constituencies and their MPs? The position holders, although most are not popular, are pivotal in the 12th parliament.

1. Bonchari Constituency – John Ooro Oyioka

Bonchari constituency comprises 4 wards, 5 locations, and 14 sublocations. The current Member of Parliament, John Ooro was elected under a PDP (People’s Democratic Party) ticket during the 2017 elections and has held the post ever since.

As usual, the Member of Parliament's responsibility for the constituency is to develop or contribute to law-making and act on behalf of the citizens he represents. The constituency has a population of around 114,000.

2. Kitutu Chache North – Jimmy Nuru Angwenyi

Jimmy is one of the Jubilee MPs in Kisii County. Although the region's Members of Parliament are not all affiliated to a single party, there is a considerable number who are part of Jubilee.

Kitutu Chache North possess 4 wards, which are Munyerero, Sensi, Marani and Mwamonari wards. The region’s population is approximately 103, 849 individuals occupying 123 Sq.Km.

The main economic activity in this constituency is small scale agricultural farming as the land is heavily subdivided out of the growing population. Many partake in small scale tea and coffee farming, dairy livestock farming, avocado farming among many other activities.

3. Kitutu Chache South - Richard Onyonka

Richard Onyonka is a member of Ford Kenya party and represents Kitutu Chache residents in parliament. Interestingly, he has been re-elected three times, consecutively using three different political parties.

Richard took over after his father, who was the Member of Parliament for the region for 34 years. The location has 5 wards (Bogusero, Bogeka, Nyakoe, Kitutu Central and Nyatieko)

4. Nyaribari Chache – Richard Nyagaka Tongi

Richard Tongi also makes the list of Jubilee MPs in Kisii constituency. He was elected under Jubilee Party in the 2017 general elections. The constituency borders Bobasi, Nyaribari Masaba, Bonchari and Kitutu Chache South constituencies.

Administratively, Nyaribari Chache possesses 6 electoral wards (Birongo, Bobaracho, Ibeno, Kheumbu, Kiogoro and Kisii Central).

5. Nyaribari Masaba – Ezekiel Machogu Ombaki

Ezekiel, a member of the National Agenda Party of Kenya (NAPK), is the legislative member of Nyaribari Masaba in parliament. The constituency has an average population of 122,000 covering an area of about 161.8 Sq. Km.

The constituency possesses fives wards which are Ichuni, Nyamasibi, Masimba, Gesusu and Kiamokama. The constituency's physical office and headquarters are at Masimba. Also, their NGO offices are located in the same region.

6. Bomachoge Chache – Miruka Ondieki Alfah

Bomachoge Chache is located in Gucha District and possesses only three wards (Boochi Tendere, Bosoti Sengera and Majoge Basi). The current Member of Parliament is a member of the KNC (Kenya National Congress) party. He won an election petition filed by Mr. Matoke.

Initially, the constituency was part of the only three constituencies in the Gucha District. However, after a comprehensive revision of national boundaries, it was split to Bomachoge, Borabu and the current Bomachoge Chache.

7. Bomachoge Borabu - Zadoc Abel Ogutu

Zadoc Abel is an independent candidate and represents Bomachoge Borabu in the 12th parliament. He is not a member of ODM MPs in Kisii County neither any other political party. Bomachoge possesses 4 wards which are Borabu Masaba, Boochi Borabu, Bokimonge and Magenche wards.

8. Bobasi – Innocent Momanyi Obiri

Bobasi constituency constitutes 8 wards which are Masige West, Masige East, Bassi Central, Nyacheki, Bassi Bogetario, Bobasi, Sameta and Boitangare. Innocent Momanyi, a member of PDP (Peoples Democratic Party) party represents this entire region in parliament.

9. South Mugirango – Hon. Silvanus Osoro Onyiego

Hon. Silvanus was elected into the 12th parliament under the KNC (Kenya National Congress) party ticket. He represents South Mugirango constituency that possesses 6 wards, 13 locations and 27 sublocations. The region covers an area of approximately 200.2 Sq.Km.

Silvanus counts as part of the list of MPs in Kisii County, massively contributing to the national development plan via different legislation in parliament.

There you have it, a comprehensive list of Kisii County constituency Members of Parliament and the regions they represent. It is evident from the data that the region has diverse political representation, which is quite interesting based on the current political culture. Nonetheless, the MPs are mandated to represent their constituents in parliament and oversee other essential roles.
