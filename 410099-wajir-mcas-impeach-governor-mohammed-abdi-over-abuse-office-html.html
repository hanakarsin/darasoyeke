Wajir MCAs Impeach Governor Mohammed Abdi Over Abuse of Office

Posted by Lourie Helzer on Tuesday, July 16, 2024

- Governor Mohamed Abdi was impeached on Tuesday, April 27, on the grounds of gross violation of the constitution, poor service delivers, and abuse of office, among other allegations

- Some 37 members of the county assembly voted in favour of the impeachment motion

- The governor's fate will now be determined by the Senate, who will subject him to grilling to ascertain the allegations levelled against him

The Wajir County Assembly members have ousted Governor Mohamed Abdi Mohamed.

Abdi was voted out by 37 members who believed he was culpable of dissipating county funds and abuse of his office.

Speaker Ibrahim Yakub, who had presided over the sitting, affirmed that two-thirds of the members had passed the motion warranting Governor Abdi's ouster.

“I the speaker of the county assembly here by declare the governor of Wajir Mohamed Abdi impeached following a unanimous voting of the MCAs in support of the same,” said the speaker.

The members also cited gross misconduct and violation of the constitution as grounds for the governor's removal.

Ten members voted to save him. Two were absent.

The Speaker of Wajir County Assembly, Ibrahim Yakub, is expected to forward deliberations of the Tuesday, April 27 impeachment to Senate Speaker Kenneth Lusaka.

Lusaka will, alongside senators, decide whether the governor will appear before a plenary committee or the entire house for grilling.

Abdi holds the impeachment was a plot by his rivals who wanted him out of office, dismissing the allegations that formed the ground for his ouster.

"The MCAs are being used by some politicians in Nairobi. Some are eyeing the Wajir top seat in 2022. I want to tell those using the county assembly to spread the gospel, that their days are numbered,” he said.

He becomes the seventh governor to be impeached since 2013, when devolution came into being.

His counterparts whos suffered the same fate include Embu's Martin Wambora, who currently chairs the Council of Governors (CoG), Kericho's Paul Chepkwony, Taita Taveta's Granton Samboja, Kiambu's Ferdinand Waititu, Kirinyaga's Anne Waiguru, and Nairobi's Mike Sonko.

Of the seven, Wambora, Chepkwony, Samboja and Waiguru survived their ousters, Sonko and Waititu having their removal upheld by the Senate.
