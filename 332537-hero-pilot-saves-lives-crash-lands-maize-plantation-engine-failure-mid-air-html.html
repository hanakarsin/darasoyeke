Hero pilot saves lives, crash lands in maize plantation after engine failure mid-air

Posted by Lourie Helzer on Thursday, June 13, 2024

- The unnamed pilot of a Cessna C-560 landed in the Otamendi Nature Reserve in the eastern Argentine province of Buenos Aires

- The plane touched down before skidding across the ground, leaving a muddy trail across the field before it stopped without injuring anyone

- The incident is under investigation by the Civil Aviation Accident Investigation Board of Argentina

A hero pilot was able to crash land safely in a maize plantation after suffering engine failure in harsh weather conditions, managing to save the lives of all nine people on board.

The unnamed pilot of a Cessna C-560 landed in the Otamendi Nature Reserve in the eastern Argentine province of Buenos Aires.

According to Daily Mail on Tuesday, December 31, the plane touched down before skidding across the ground, leaving a muddy trail across the field before it stopped without injuring anyone.

The business jet had departed from San Fernando International Airport and was on its way to La Venancia, a private airstrip in Otamendi.
