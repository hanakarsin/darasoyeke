KRA Collects Record KSh 144b in March, Surpasses Target By KSh 6.6b

Posted by Almeda Bohannan on Friday, May 3, 2024

- This is the highest figure recorded by the taxman in the 2020-2021 financial year

- Customs and Border Control collections in March increased to KSh 60.751 billion

- The Pay As You Earn (PAYE) increased by KSh 3.339 billion in March, hitting KSh 34.595 billion

Kenya Revenue Authority (KRA) collected KSh 144.6 billion revenue in March 2021, despite the tough economic times the country is facing.

This is the highest figure recorded by the taxman in the 2020-2021 financial year as it surpassed its March target by KSh 6.6 billion, a performance rate of 105.1%.

The authority's commissioner-general Githii Mburu said that was an outstanding performance compared to KSh 127.7 billion collected in February 2021.

For the fourth month in a row, KRA posted an above-the-target performance since December 2020, thanks to the enhanced compliance efforts and improved service delivery to taxpayers.

The tax agency, in a statement, indicated that the Customs and Border Control collections in March increased to KSh 60.751 billion compared to KSh 51.3 billion collected in February.

The Pay As You Earn (PAYE) increased by KSh 3.339 billion, hitting KSh 34.595 billion.

Value Added Tax (VAT) collections amounted to KSh 17.017 billion, which is a performance rate of 81.3%

Digital service tax

In a separate story, the newly introduced digital service tax which is payable on income accrued from services through the digital marketplace in Kenya, became effective on January 1, and signals tough times ahead for online players.

Through the Finance Act, 2020, it was brought into law by President Uhuru Kenyatta and will seek to boost the dwindling government revenues amid the COVID-19 pandemic.

Some of the services that will be charged under the digital tax include downloadable digital content such as mobile apps, e-books and films, and over-the-top services, including streaming television shows, films, music, podcasts and any other digital content.
