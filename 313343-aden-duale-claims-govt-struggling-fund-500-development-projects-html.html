Aden Duale claims govt struggling to fund over 500 development projects

Posted by Hettie Henneman on Sunday, August 11, 2024

- Duale said he had tabled in the National Assembly a list of 545 stalled projects with an estimated cost of KSh 365.9 billion

- He argued the development projects stalled because of financial challenges the national government was grappling with

- The MP ruled out slashing budget allocation to the executive as standoff over revenue sharing with county governments escalated

National Assembly Majority leader Aden Duale has claimed the national government is struggling to finance hundreds of mega development projects due to inadequate budgetary allocation.

Duale, who had strongly objected to governors and senators' demand for additional revenue allocation to counties, disclosed the executive arm of the government was equally cash-strapped, with over 500 projects stalled due to budget deficit.

In a thread of tweets, the fiery Garissa Town legislator indicated he had tabled in the National Assembly a list of stalled development projects with an estimated cost of over KSh 360 billion.

"This House is aware of the list of stalled projects I tabled in this House sometimes back. That list revealed that a total of 545 projects with an estimated cost of KSh 365.9 billion had stalled.The projects comprised of an estimated GoK funding of KSh 286.9 billion and foreign financing of KSh 78.9 billion," he tweeted on Tuesday, August 20.

The MP argued due to the financial challenges the executive was grappling with and the many pending projects yet to be completed, reducing budget allocation to the said arm of government would be unacceptable.

"Reducing the budget for the national executive is not tenable. Already the executive has serious challenges implementing projects that they have started due to inadequate budgetary allocation," he said.

The executive was allocated a total of about KSh 1.84 trillion in the KSh 3 trillion national budget for the financial year 2019-2020.

County governments were allocated KSh 316 billion, up from KSh 310 billion that had initially been approved for the 2019/2020 fiscal year.

Senators and governors wanted KSh 335 billion, which has since been reduced to KSh 327 amid a deadly standoff between Senate and the National Assembly over the 2019 Division of Revenue Bill.

The National Assembly opposed the governors' push for KSh 335 billion revenue allocation and proposed KSh 316 billion, a proposal that was rejected by the senators.

Talks between the county bosses and the executive failed to break the stalemate with President Uhuru Kenyatta insisting there is no money to add governors.
