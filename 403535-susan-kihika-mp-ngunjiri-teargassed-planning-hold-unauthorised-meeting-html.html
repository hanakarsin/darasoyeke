Susan Kihika, MP Ngunjiri and others teargassed for planning to hold unauthorised meeting

Posted by Hettie Henneman on Tuesday, April 2, 2024

- Ruto's UDA supporters were expected to convene a political rally in London ward to drum up support for by-election candidate Wachira

- The politicians led by Senator Kihika and MP Ngunjiri Kimani attempted to make their way to the venue but got stopped by police

- The officers claimed the planned public gathering was in contravention of the Public Order Act

A cloud of teargas has welcomed politicians allied to Deputy President William Ruto's United Democratic Alliance (UDA) during a visit to London ward in Nakuru county.

A heavy convoy of high end vehicles carrying Ruto's Nakuru right hand man and Bahati MP Kimani Ngunjri and Senator Susan Kihika snaked its way through the county to London ward.

The two who were in the company of youthful Dagoretti South MP John Kiarie alias KJ and Nyali's Mohamed Ali were expected to convene a campaign rally to drum up support for Nzuki Wachira.

Wachira was the man Tanga Tanga wing of the Jubilee Party through UDA entrusted with its flag in the upcoming March 4 London ward by-election.

The grand entry of Ngunjiri and company was met by a heavy contingent of police officers who barricaded the main road leading to the venue on Saturday, February 20.

A small altercation between the host politicians and the police officers ensued over the illegality of the planned meeting with each side maintaining it was abiding by the Public Order Act.

"We gave the notice to hold this meeting today. A female officer, that Kaptembwe OCS has that letter and she has refused to produce it. We have a video of her receiving the letter," Kihika said.

Illegal meeting

After minutes of trying to convince the officers that they indeed had been issued with permit to hold the meeting as per the law, the politicians gave up and walked away but soon it was all smoke.

Other by-elections

The seat fell vacant following the passing away of MCA Samuel Karanja Mbugua who succumbed to suspected COVID-19 complications on November 18, 2020.

Other by-elections slated for the same day (March 4) include the election of Matungu MP, members of county assemblies of Huruma, Hell’s Gate, Kiamokama and Kitise/Kithuku Wards.
