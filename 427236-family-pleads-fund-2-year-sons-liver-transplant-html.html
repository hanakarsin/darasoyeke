Family Pleads for Help to Fund 2-Year-Old Son's Liver Transplant in India

Posted by Chauncey Koziol on Wednesday, July 31, 2024
  • December 5, 2018, is a special day for Morgan Muchera and Faith Shisiali as that's the day their lovely boy Baby Muchera Zarrel was born
  • Zarrel grew up as a normal kid, and his parents were elated to see him play football in the house
  • However, at two years, the boy became ill and was diagnosed with liver cancer
  • The family is now pleading with wellwishers to help them raise funds to get the required treatment for their son

On December 5, 2018, a happy couple, Morgan Muchera and Faith Shisiali welcomed their firstborn baby Muchera Zarrel into their family.

As he grew up, the little boy hit all his milestones well and loved to play football in the house.

But one day in December last year, Zarrel's tummy began to swell. The worried parents rushed him to the hospital to determine the cause of this.

Liver cancer

It was then that doctors informed them that their son had hepatoblastoma malignant of the liver.

This rare tumour starts in cells in the liver and is the most common cancerous liver tumour in babies.

"Zarrel started chemotherapy straight away. In May, he underwent two successful operations to remove the tumors. But last month it recurred and the doctors advised us to book a liver transplant. This can only be done in India," Zarrels father Morgan Muchera told TUKO.co.ke in a phone interview.

He said that the family has spent close to KSh 2.5 million to treat this condition before it recurred.

Heartbroken dad says son has become dull

He is heartbroken to see his once lively and playful boy reduced to just a quiet, docile baby. He said he would do anything to see his son back to his former self.

Mom seeks help to care for two mentally ill kids

Elsewhere a distressed single mom is pleading for help to take care of her two mentally challenged kids.

Florence Wangui says her two kids were born normal, but as they grew up, they have been in and out of the hospital countless times for various illnesses.

In a phone interview with TUKO.co.ke, Wangui said that she is singlehandedly fending for her babies after their father fled due to the sicknesses.
