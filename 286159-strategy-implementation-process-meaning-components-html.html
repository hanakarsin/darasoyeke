Strategy implementation process: meaning and components

Posted by Lourie Helzer on Friday, June 21, 2024

Strategy implementation process is one way by which organizational objectives, strategies, and policies are put into action through the development of programs, budgets, and procedures. The organization is bound to fail in its goals if proper and effective strategies are not formulated and implemented. When about to implement a strategic plan, one inevitably confronts problems and risks such as the possibility of wasting limited resources, or the threat to self-esteem if one fails. Planning thus prepares one to overcome unexpected difficulties and to work in the most effective ways of reaching the set targets.

Businesses are known to formulate excellent strategies but still do not attain competitive advantage and sustainability. Do you know why? This is mostly attributed to the fact that even with excellently formulated strategies, an organization will not realize its goals and objectives if these strategies are not well executed. This is why this article will focus on the strategy implementation process to help you put into action all the things required for the formulated strategy to be of benefit to the organization.

Characteristics of strategy implementation process

The strategic implementation process is of the following nature

  • Strategy implementation is basically an operational process.
  • For implementation to be done, there should be coordination among many individuals.
  • Special motivation and leadership skills are needed.
  • It concentrates on managing forces during action as well as on efficiency.

Components of strategy implementation process

The key elements in the strategy implementation process in a business include:

1. Organization

For successful implementation, you have to make sure that your strategy matches the type of organization.

2. Planning

Implementation of the strategy involves a series of activities. The activities should be well organized in a schedule i.e. properly planned.

3. Resources

Ensure you have the right people and resources to complete the implementation of the strategies.

4. Communication

Communicating the details of the strategy in the organization is essential. Written descriptions, procedures, images and drawings can serve as a basis for team meetings to answer questions, review progress, and find solutions to problems.

5. Monitoring

To avoid the deviation of results from the plan, ensure all the activities are on schedule and budget.

The process of strategy implementation

The following five steps describe the strategy implementation process:

  • Align your initiatives: Once you have formulated the strategy and selected the best strategy to be implemented, stop doing things the old way. Activities of the organization should be renewed so that they can be relevant to the new strategy.
  • Align budgets and performance: Each department of the organization should manage their budgets so as to contribute to the strategic initiatives. Also, the performance of the firm should be aligned closely to the selected strategy by placing performance measures against strategic goals.
  • Structure aligned to strategy: Ensure that the organizational structure allows efficient and meaningful delivery of the strategy for transformation to occur in the organization. An outdated organization structure will hinder the successful implementation of the new strategy.
  • Engaging workers and critical stakeholders: Employees and key stakeholders should be aware of their role towards the attainment of the formulated strategy. Communicate the strategic vision again and again to minimize resistance to its implementation. Involve all players and clarify expectations from each individual.
  • Monitor and adapt: The strategy should be flexible and adaptable to respond to the ever-changing internal and external business environments. Performance and strategic relevance should be reviewed regularly to ensure that the organization is on the right track.

    Implementation process of marketing strategy

    Marketing is the backbone of any organization as it brings customers to the organization, increasing sales and enabling the firm realize its objectives such as profit maximization. Here are steps to help you successfully execute your marketing strategy.

  • Establish correct expectations: Marketing is not a quick fix, therefore, the returns of the marketing efforts will take some time to be seen. Expectations set should be realistic.
  • Build the marketing team and secure resource: Come up with a marketing implementation team that will help in the marketing effort. You can use internal marketing experts or outsource a marketing partner to aid you. Other than human resource required to implement the marketing strategy, you should have the proper tools, equipment, and materials to execute the strategy.
  • Communicate the strategy: With the necessary resources in place, inform the marketing team about the strategy so that they understand its goals and the role of each individual in the implementation effort. Also, tell other members of the firm about the marketing efforts so that they support where they can.
  • Establish timeline and activities: To make the marketing effort manageable, break each project into smaller tasks and set deadlines, This way, you will be able to implement the entire marketing strategy easily without making employees feel overwhelmed.
  • Establish measures for success: Come up with appropriate tracking tools to measure your marketing efforts. The measurements should be based on the goals of the marketing strategy.
  • Monitor: Regularly examine the results from the measurements above to know if the marketing efforts are working or not. Meet with the marketing team and discuss the progress so that you can know if you are on the right track.
  • Be flexible and adapt to changes: If a certain marketing effort is not giving the desired outcome, come up with ways to improve the tactic for it to bear results.
  • Communicate results: Let the team know that their efforts are working. This way, they will be motivated to continue working towards the attainment of the marketing strategy.

    Common problems experienced in strategy implementation

    • An implementation may take more time than originally planned.
    • The information system inadequately monitored activities.
    • Poor definition of key implementation tasks and activities.
    • Lack of adequate leadership and direction from departmental managers.
    • Uncontrollable factors in the external environment pose challenges, for example, political instability.
    • Inadequately training for the lower level employees.
    • The employees tasked with implementing the strategies may have limited capabilities.
    • Competing activities may take attention off the implementation process.
    • Major problems arising from uncertainties.
    • Poor coordination of activities in the organization during the strategy implementation process.

    Summary of steps in the strategy implementation process

  • Evaluation and communication of the strategic plan.
  • Development of an implementation structure.
  • Development of implementation support policies and programs.
  • Budgeting and allocation of resources.
  • Discharge of functions and activities.
