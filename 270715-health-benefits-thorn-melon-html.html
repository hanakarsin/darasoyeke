Health benefits of thorn melon (kiwano or horned melon)

Posted by Almeda Bohannan on Monday, April 1, 2024

Thorn melons are cultivated through farming, but they also grow by themselves in the bush and fields. The plant germinates well at an altitude between two hundred and ten and one thousand eight hundred above sea level. Thorn melons are also planted in greenhouses because of their high market value. The thorn melon is locally known as kiwano, passion mwitu, jelly melon, para chichi mwitu, African horned cucumber, or passion lenye tete maji. A ripe thorn melon is orange in color while its internal part is lime green in color. This article will cover the nutritional benefits of thorn melon in the body.

Benefits of thorn melon

Kiwano fruit has numerous health benefits, and Africans consider it to be a symbol of long life because of its nutrients and medicinal attributes. In fact, thorn melon is quite famous and praised by obese individuals and people with arthritis and diabetes. Have you ever wondered what are the benefits of eating thorn melon?

Well, some of the advantages of thorn melon in the body include weight loss, boosting metabolism, protection of the skin, prevention of some chronic diseases, strengthening of the bones, enhancement of eye health, quickens healing of wounds, improves cognitive ability as well as optimizing digestion.

Below is a list of health benefits of thorn melon:

1.Weight loss is one of the benefits of thorn melon in the body

Thorn melon has a water content of almost eighty percent, making it perfect for filling the stomach without adding any pounds. The fruit is highly advisable for persons trying to lose weight because it has fiber and nutrients that prevent one from feeling hungry fast. Kiwano is low in fats and calories; therefore, it is consumed mostly by people who have issues with their weight.

2. Thorn melon benefits in eye health

Apart from weight loss, another one of the health benefits of thorn melon fruit is promoting eye health. The content of vitamin A is very essential in Thorn Melon which makes it a valuable fruit and booster for the eyes. Also, the Vitamin A Carotene types found in the kiwano fruit prevents cataracts from developing and gets rid of free radicals that result in macular degeneration

3.Thorn melon fruit boosts metabolism and is a stress-reliever

Horned melon also has benefits to metabolic health. The fruit has an element of Zinc, a mineral that plays a crucial responsibility in metabolism and produces the protein essential for healing wounds and repairing tissues, organs, blood vessels, and cells. Research has also indicated that Kiwano can be utilized in easing the mind and get the body to a relaxed and calm state.

4. Thorn melon prevents abnormalities in a fetus and anemia

For expectant mothers, thorn melon is not only fulfilling but jam-packed with minerals and vitamins needed during the pregnancy. Benefits of thorn melon during pregnancy include preventing amnesia, leg cramps, and abnormalities in a fetus as well as boosting the immune system, just to name a few.

The high amount of Vitamin C in Kiwano plays a crucial role in the assimilation of iron which is vital during pregnancy due to the fact that apart from forming the red blood cells, it also develops tissues for both the placenta and the baby. As a result, thorn melons are highly advisable during pregnancy to prevent anemia which causes infant mortality and premature labor.

In most cases, expectant women often suffer from leg cramps usually a sign of potassium shortage intake. Pregnant women approximately need 4.1 mg of potassium daily achieved by taking one kilogram of thorn melon instead. Also, thorn melon has a high amount of vitamin B9 or folic acid that contributes significantly to the early development of an unborn baby.

Furthermore, melon has zinc that aids in the development of the body's tissues. Based on studies, the possibility of neural tube defects in a fetus can quickly be reduced up to 50 percent when an expectant woman consumes thorn melon daily.

5. Thorn melon fruit is an antioxidant natural source

Thorn melon contains antioxidants that are beneficial to one's health. Kiwano has high levels of alpha-tocopherol compounds that are potent antioxidant forms of Vitamin E. These antioxidant benefits are crucial for the health of blood vessels and nerves. Medical benefits of thorn melon also include neutralizing free radicals that trigger heart diseases and degenerative cancer.

6. The fruit can improve cognitive ability and bone strength

The composition of vitamin E in Kiwano is of great use in cognitive health and is directly connected to slowing the onset of dementia and Alzheimer's disease. Thorn melon has high levels of tocopherol which are mainly used to maintain the health of the brain and keeps the minds sharp and fresh. Kiwanos is also famous for high mineral content; especially calcium utilized in the prevention of the onset of osteoporosis and boost bone strength, growth, and repair.

7. Thorn melon helps with digestion

Thorn melon has a high dietary fiber composition that assists in the digestion process. Dietary fiber aids to clear out the gastrointestinal tract and stimulates peristaltic motion, keeping bowel movements regular and preventing bloating, cramping, constipation, and severe conditions such as colon cancer and gastric ulcers. Dietary fiber also aids in the regulation of the level of cholesterol in the human body and insulin receptors, which prevents or manages diabetes.
