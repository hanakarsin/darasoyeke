Congo: International Community Warns of Second Volcanic Eruption, Humanitarian Crisis as Residents F

Posted by Almeda Bohannan on Wednesday, April 10, 2024

- Authorities in the DRC have ordered the evacuation of a portion of the city of Goma due to the threat of more eruptions from volcano Nyiragongo,

- The order has resulted in a mass migration of tens of thousands of people

- Authorities said they could not rule out an eruption on land or beneath Lake Kivu, which may happen any day from now

- According to local officials in the eastern DR Congo region, toxic gases dissolved under Lake Kivu posed a risk of destabilisation

The International Rescue Committee (IRC) has warned of a second volcanic eruption risk in the Democratic Republic of Congo (DRC)’s Goma city.

The committee raised the concerned hours after the Military Governor of North Kivu Constant Ndima Kongba announced a partial evacuation of Goma earlier on Thursday, May 27.

Ndima said the molten rock that normally stays beneath the earth's crust had been detected beneath the city and the adjoining Lake Kivu, citing seismic and ground deformation data.

"Given these scientific observations, an eruption on land or under the lake cannot be ruled out at present, and it could occur with very little or no warning," he said.

The DRC Country Director at IRC, Borry Jatta, said the impact of the eruption is not behind the people of Goma noting there have been more than 200 tremors in the past two days.

“Researchers observing the volcano have a limited sense of the current risk level; the situation remains highly unpredictable as several warning signs emerge of the risk of a new eruption in the next few days," he said.

Gas emissions, acidic rain and volcanic dust also continue to pose health risks to the population in the affected area.

"Multiple cracks on the ground, emission of gas and the worrying fact that the Kivu crater seems to have refilled very quickly, indicating another possible eruption," added Jatta.

He further noted the IRC was preparing a response to meet the escalating humanitarian needs in collaboration with the government and other NGOs.

"There is now a desperate need for an increase in funding through support from the international community," he said.
