Yoweri Museveni vows to remain in power until he identifies worth leader for Uganda

Posted by Lourie Helzer on Monday, July 1, 2024

- Museveni said Kiiza Besigye, Major General Mugisha Muntu and Lieutenant General Henry Tumunkunde were all unfit to replace him

- He claimed the three potential heirs had failed to convince him that they were able to lead Uganda to the next level

- Museveni took over power in 1986 and will be seeking his sixth term in office in the upcoming General Election

- He is facing stiff competition from Bobi Wine, the 38-year-old National Unity Platform (NUP) presidential candidate

Uganda's President Yoweri Museveni has vowed to remain at the helm of the country's leadership until that time he will personally identify a suitable presidential candidate to take over from him.

Museveni who has been in power since 1986 said none of his competitors in the upcoming general election has demonstrated ability and capacity to led the country.

"They have failed"

Speaking during a campaign rally in Rukungiri District on Monday, December 14, the 76-year-old politician said he would not leave power to people like former presidential candidate Kiiza Besigye or his current competitors, Major General Mugisha Muntu and Lieutenant General Henry Tumunkunde.

Besigye, Mugisha and Tumunkunde participated in the five-year guerrilla war that propelled Museveni to power.

"They have failed to show their usefulness when I'm still around and you want me to leave the country for them? I will stay around until I identify someone who is worth people for whom I can leave the country," said the National Resistance Movement (NRM) leader.

Museveni will be seeking to extend his leadership to 39 years in the upcoming election slated for January 14, 2021.

Harassing competitors

The Ugandan leader is facing stiff competition from MP Robert Kyangulani alias Bobi Wine, the 38-year-old National Unity Platform (NUP) presidential candidate.

The NRM leader has been accused of using the police to harass and intimidate his competitors.

On several occasions, political rallies organised by Wine have been disrupted by security forces or at some point, his convoy blocked from accessing the venue.

As earlier reported by TUKO.co.ke, the NUP presidential candidate has severally been arrested, beaten up by police officers and even forced to sleep by the roadside.

On Sunday, FDC presidential candidate Patrick Amuriat had his convoy was blocked on the road and further denied access to a fuel station.

Frustrated by the turn of events, Amuriat walked out of his car and sat on the road barefooted in protest to the unending harassment by police officers.
