President Obama Gives Talk At Kenyatta University!

Posted by Enda Jantzen on Monday, January 1, 0001

US President Barack Obama met with civil society leaders at Kenyatta University on Sunday, July 26, 2015.

14:56 Talk concluded at Kenyatta University.

14:55 Obama, "the US and Kenya have so much in common and so much shared history that the areas of disagreement are far fewer than the areas where we can work togethor."

14:54 US will promote the kinds of policies they believe in, Obama.

14:52 Says that the US will support both government and NGOs, says America will "speak out" against suppression.

14:51 Says "we will look suspiciously on laws that say peaceful groups can't operate because they are critical of the government, for example."

14:49 "You have a constitution, you should abide by your constitution."

14:47 "The challenge that we would have as an outside party as the USA, is that how that plays itself out within Kenya is up to the Kenyan people." Obama on devolution.

After giving a speech at Kasarani Indoor Arena to a packed crowd of more than 5,000 people, Obama headed towards the campus of Kenyatta University (KU).

The principal reason for the visit to KU is for Obama to open the Young African Leaders Initiative (YALI). The YALI program seeks to train leaders, increase support for entrepreneurship and to bolster networking and professional development. The program will further assist young people to connect with capital.

Obama is also expected to briefly meet with leaders from Kenyan civil society.
