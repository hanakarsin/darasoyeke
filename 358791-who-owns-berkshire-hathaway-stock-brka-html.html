Who owns the most Berkshire Hathaway stock (BRK.A)?

Posted by Almeda Bohannan on Monday, July 15, 2024

Who are the owners of Berkshire Hathaway? If you keep up to date with stock trading, you’ve probably heard the name, Berkshire Hathaway. And if not, you have perhaps heard the company mentioned in one setting or another. But while it is famous, it’s owners might not be a household name to many people. So, who owns the most Berkshire Hathaway stock?

Since it is a publicly listed company, Berkshire Hathaway's ownership is shared among stockholders who own the company's shares. However, the major shareholders are the movers and shakers where the company's interests are concerned.

Berkshire Hathaway company history

Before we look at the owners, it is good to understand the history behind the company. Its roots can be traced back to 1839 when a businessman by the name Oliver Chance founded Valley Falls Company, a textile manufacturing company in Valley Falls, Rhode Islands.

Nearly a century later, Valley Falls would merge with Berkshire Textile Manufacturing Company leading to Berkshire Spinning Associates. In 1955 another merger occurred, this time with Hathaway Manufacturing Company, leading to today’s Berkshire Hathaway.

However, it would take a long time before the company transformed from into its current form. The transformation all started in 1962 when Warren Buffett began buying its stock. In 1965, he acquired control of the company, which had been mostly involved in the textile industry. Two years later, he purchased National Indemnity, an insurance company, starting its transformation into a conglomerate.

Today, Berkshire Hathaway is a multinational holding company with interests in multiple sectors: insurance, railroads, apparel, jewellery, furniture, jet rentals, media, and more. It has its headquarters in Omaha, Nebraska, and Warren Buffet is its current CEO and chairman of the board of directors.

Current Berkshire Hathaway shareholders

Now that you know the company's history, here is a look at who owns the most of Berkshire Hathaway stock.

1. Warren Buffett

How much Berkshire Hathaway stock does Warren Buffett Own? Buffett and Berkshire Hathaway are synonymous. Since taking over the company in 1965, Buffett has used it as his investment vehicle. He is considered one of the top investors in the world, with his transformation of Berkshire Hathaway from a textile company to a multinational conglomerate being a testament to this fact.

Aside from being a successful investor and businessman, he is also one of the wealthiest people in the world, with a net worth of $68.7 billion. Most of his wealth is invested in Berkshire Hathaway, making him one of the top shareholders. He is estimated to own about 35.91% of the company. If you were wondering who owns the most Berkshire Hathaway stock, then it is Warren Buffet.

2. The Vanguard Group, Inc.

A large number of Berkshire Hathaway shareholders are not individuals but institutions. The Vanguard Group, Inc., is another one of the top owners of the company. It is an investment management company based in Malvern, Pennsylvania, US.

It is considered one of the largest mutual funds companies in the world with an AUM (assets under management) of $6.2 trillion as of January 2020. The company owns a 10.00% stake at Berkshire Hathaway.

3. BlackRock Fund Advisors

BlackRock Fund Advisors is another top investment management firm that owns Berkshire Hathaway shares. The multinational corporation is based in New York City, USA, and is one of the leading investment companies with assets under management totalling $7.4 trillion as of 2019. It has a stake of 5.82%.

4. SSgA Funds Management, Inc.

SSGA Funds Management, Inc. is an investment management firm that operates as a division of State Street Corporation. It offers services to various institutions and organizations. It has an impressive portfolio with a total of US $2.8 trillion worth of assets under management as of 2017. It has a 5.53% stake at Berkshire Hathaway.

5. Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation Trust

Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation Trust is another institutional shareholder. The trust manages the endowment assets of the Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation, a private foundation that is involved in philanthropic activities in the US and around the world. As of 2018, it managed a total of $46.8 billion of endowment assets. It has a 3.25% stake in Berkshire Hathaway.

Berkshire Hathaway stock

In addition to looking at the top owners, it is also vital to gain a bit of understanding of the Berkshire Hathaway stocks. The company has both Class A and Class B shares. The Berkshire Hathaway stock class A has a current price of US $263,094.

With most company shares going for tens or hundreds of dollars, this is quite a high price. So, why Berkshire Hathaway stock is so expensive? Well, there are two reasons for this. The first one is the company's consistent growth, which has seen the Class A stock price rise from $265 in 1980 to the current price.

The second reason is that the company has never split its Class A shares. Splitting of shares occurs when a company divides the existing stock to increase liquidity, usually to raise capital. Under Warren Buffett's leadership, Berkshire Hathaway has never split the Class A shares, and therefore their value has never dropped beyond the market fluctuations.

On the other hand, Berkshire Hathaway stock B price is a little lower, with the current price standing at $174.93. However, compared to other companies on the S&P 500 list, the stock is quite strong, a testament to its financial standing.
