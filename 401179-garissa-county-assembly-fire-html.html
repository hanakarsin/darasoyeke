Garissa county assembly on fire

Posted by Almeda Bohannan on Wednesday, August 21, 2024

- The fire broke out at around 8:20am on Thursday, January 28

- County clerk Mohamud Santur termed the damage caused by the inferno as "extensive"

- According to MCAs who spoke to the press, at least 48 offices were affected by the fire.

- Some of the offices affected included that of the auditor, assembly staff, Sacco, payroll, finance, committee meeting rooms as well as several chairperson offices

Concerted efforts are underway to put off a huge fire that is razing down Garissa county assembly.

The cause of the inferno which engulfed the county premise remains unknown.

Photos and video clips that surfaced on social media pages showed a huge smoke billowing into the sky as the fire razed the roof of the vast building.

Firefighters, police officer, officials from the Red Cross Society and locals are currently striving to contain the inferno which reportedly broke out at around 8:20am.

Extensive damage

Speaking to the press, county clerk Mohamud Santur termed the damage caused by the fire outbreak as "extensive".

He estimated the damage to be about 40% of the whole structure and property therein.

"We cannot ascertain the exact damage at this point but as you can see it is extensive, about 40% of the whole structure has been destroyed. We will give an update on the exact damage and cause of the fire after investigations are completed," said the county official.

Some of the county workers who were around when the outbreak occurred indicated an electrical fault could be the primary cause.

The inferno reportedly started at the Hansard and swiftly spread to the chambers.

Crucial offices

According to MCAs who spoke to the press, at least 48 offices were affected by the fire.

Assembly minority leader Mohamed Ali said although they managed to secure some crucial documents, the offices that were damaged were important to the day to day running of the county.

Some of the offices affected included that of the auditor, assembly staff, Sacco, payroll, finance, committee meeting rooms as well as several chairperson offices

Cases of fire outbreak in county offices are not new. In September 2017, Homa Bay county governor's office was razed down under unclear circumstances.

The office caught fire barely days after officers from the Ethics and Anti Corruption Commission (EACC) raided it and carted away crucial documents and electronics.

In September 2020, Kisii county assembly also reported a fire outbreak incident amid graft investigations.
