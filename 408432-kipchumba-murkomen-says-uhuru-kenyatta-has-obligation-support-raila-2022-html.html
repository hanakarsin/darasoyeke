Kipchumba Murkomen Says Uhuru Kenyatta Has Obligation to Support Raila in 2022

Posted by Hettie Henneman on Friday, August 16, 2024

- Elgeyo Marakwet Senator Kipchumba Murkomen argued that President Uhuru Kenyatta was obliged to support ODM party leader Raila Odinga in the coming 2022 General Election

- Murkomen stated that it would be unfair for Uhuru to shortchange Raila since the late Daniel Moi also did that to him

- He also said that hustlers already have their preferred candidate for the 2022 General Election; hence they did not need anyone from outside

Elgeyo Marakwet Senator Kipchumba Murkomen has challenged President Uhuru Kenyatta fulfil his promise of supporting ODM leader Raila Odinga in 2022.

Taking to social media on Sunday, April 11, Murkomen stated that it would be a mistake of monumental proportion for Uhuru to shortchange Raila when he knows that the late president Daniel Arap Moi abandoned Raila for his sake.

"It will be a mistake of monumental proportion for Uhuru Kenyatta to shortchange Raila Odinga when he knows that Daniel Arap Moi shortchanged him (Tinga) for his (Uhuru’s) sake. He must stand by his decision through BBI and support Raila in 2022. Hustlers already have their own plan," he wrote.

The senator argued that Uhuru must stand by his decision through Building Bridges Initiative (BBI) and support Raila in 2022, saying the hustlers already have their preferred candidate for the 2022 General Election thus don't need anyone from outside.

Recently rumours have been going around about the fate of the famous handshake between Uhuru and Raila, with many claiming that it was slowly going down while others sensing betrayal at the end.

In March, the ODM Chairman John Mbadi hinted that Uhuru might betray the former premier in 2022 despite their famous handshake.

The Suba South MP, a close ally of the ODM leader, said betrayal was normal in politics and insisted the opposition outfit was ready for anything.

"Mt Kenya will play a key role in the succession politics. But we are already immune to emotions and politics of betrayal. Uhuru needs Raila for the handshake objectives to come true,” said Mbadi.

Mbadi said the success or failure of the handshake depended on the two principals.

The recent nine-hour meeting between Kakamega governor Wycliffe Oparanya and Deputy President William Ruto in Mara has also raised eyebrows.

Many think that the meeting was for possible coalition formation between Raila and Ruto.

However, in a press conference on Sunday, April 11, Bahati MP Kimani Ngunjiri rubbished the claims that the meeting between Oparanya and the deputy president was premised on the possibility of a coalition between the two leaders.

The Tanga Tanga lawmaker insisted the DP cannot work with the former prime minister because the now seemingly flimsy handshake truce between him and Uhuru only proved he was not good with political marriages.
